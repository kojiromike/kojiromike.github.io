Brand-Driven Development

I hate brands. Well, actually I love brands, but I hate that I love them, you know? Brands are the face in front of those manipulative marketing schemes like Facebook's emotion-tampering experiment[1], the discontinuity behind Vans Star Wars shoes (what?) and quite frequently the only actual value add for what would otherwise be a commodity product. (I'm like yo, that's $50 for a t-shirt.) Brands aren't just for commerce though. Now we have personal brands and we even brand programming paradigms and development practices. Branding development practices is important because it helps us communicate the value of the practice to businesspeople (read: our bosses) when we can't communicate the more technical ideas behind it. That's a powerful capability for non-technical people, but it's dangerous for technologists – brands are always more valuable for the producer than for the recipient.

A brand provides a platform for software, literature, media and personal expertise in a subject

The brand of a technology paradigm such as "Agile" project management can lead to brittle Brands create value = books, automatons; rules and rigid/brittle processes

Brands are more valuable for the people spreading them than for the people receiving.

Brands as a mode of communication / mass thought/ meme makes it harder for the brand to evolve. I say "Test Driven Development" and you think I'm doing something from 1995, but I have a very different thing in mind.

As a black-box abstraction for good ideas, brands provide value.
